# How to Care for Your Dental Implants: A Complete Guide

Dental implants are widely considered the gold standard for replacing missing teeth. They look, feel, and function just like natural teeth. One of the greatest benefits of dental implants is their longevity; with proper care, they can last a lifetime.

However, just like natural teeth, implants require diligent oral hygiene. While implants themselves cannot get cavities, the surrounding gums and bone can still get infected if plaque is allowed to build up. This condition is known as **peri-implantitis**, and it is the leading cause of implant failure.

In this guide, we will walk you through the best practices for keeping your dental implants clean, healthy, and long-lasting.

## 1. Brush Twice a Day with a Soft-Bristled Brush

You should brush your implants at least twice a day, just like your natural teeth. Here are a few tips:
- **Use a soft brush:** Medium or hard bristles can scratch the porcelain crown or damage your gums.
- **Consider an electric toothbrush:** Electric toothbrushes are highly effective at removing plaque from hard-to-reach areas around the implant.
- **Use low-abrasive toothpaste:** Avoid highly abrasive whitening toothpastes, as they can wear down the glaze on your porcelain crown.

## 2. Floss Daily (Yes, Even Implants!)

Plaque can easily accumulate in the tiny gap between the implant crown and the gum line. Flossing is critical to clean these areas:
- **Implant-safe floss:** Use a thick, spongy dental floss or specialized implant floss designed to wrap around the base of the implant.
- **Water Flossers:** A water flosser (like a Waterpik) is an excellent tool for implant patients. The pressurized stream of water easily flushes out food particles and bacteria from under the crown without irritating the gums.

## 3. Avoid Smoking

Smoking is one of the most common causes of dental implant failure. Nicotine restricts blood flow to the gums and bone, slowing down the healing process and weakening the bone that supports the implant. If you have implants, quitting smoking is one of the best things you can do to protect your investment.

## 4. Visit Your Dentist Every 6 Months

Even with perfect home care, professional cleanings are essential. During your [Preventive Care](/treatments/preventive-care) visits at One Smile Dental Care, we use specialized tools made of plastic or resin (rather than metal) to clean around the titanium implants. This ensures we remove calcified tartar without scratching the implant structure.

We will also take periodic digital X-rays to monitor the bone levels around your implant, ensuring it remains securely anchored.
